# The Well

**The Well** is a mobile-first wellness and cultural identity app built around voice, reflection, story, and ancestral memory. It is designed for Black and diaspora users who want a wellness space that does not ask them to translate themselves first.

The current prototype is a fully functional single-file mobile web app. It is also the foundation for a proposed XR experience that extends the water type system and story engine into an immersive environment, where the user's emotional identity shapes the space around them in real time.

---

## The problem it addresses

Most wellness and mental health tools are built on frameworks that assume a culturally neutral user. They are not neutral. They reflect a particular set of assumptions about how people process emotion, relate to the body, speak about difficulty, and understand the self.

For Black and diaspora users, the result is a persistent gap between what these tools offer and what is actually needed. The data on this is consistent: Black, Indigenous, and racialised communities are underserved by existing mental health infrastructure, face higher barriers to access, and are less likely to find tools that reflect their emotional and cultural reality.

The Well is a direct response to that gap.

---

## Core concept

At the centre of the app is a water type system. Users discover which kind of water they are: Still, Running, Deep, Restless, Tidal, Spring, or Underground. These are not personality categories. They are emotional rhythms that describe how a person processes, protects, speaks, and returns to themselves.

The water type system is the connective tissue of the app. It shapes the language users receive, the prompts they are offered, the stories they move through, and the way the tool responds to them. The experience is designed to feel less like assessment and more like recognition.

---

## Current prototype features

The prototype is a complete mobile web app including:

**Speak.** Voice and written journaling with prompts shaped by water type and emotional state.

**Listen.** A curated space for diaspora essays and audio stories, with reaction and save functionality.

**Wander.** A cultural knowledge sphere organised by region and theme, drawing on practices, philosophies, folktales, histories, and ways of living from across the African diaspora and the Global South.

**Play.** Reflective tools including an interactive branching story engine, emotional mapping, daily questions, and breath-based exercises.

**The Well Speaks.** A branching story engine where choices accumulate quietly across scenes, and the ending reflects the user back to themselves without labelling or explaining their choices. Current story threads are set in present-day Accra, Gold Coast 1900, Benin City 1896, and southeastern Nigeria 1929. Each historical thread opens with a factual note grounding the player in the real events they are moving through.

**Breathe.** Grounding exercises framed through water and body rather than generic wellness language.

**Future Self.** A letter-writing feature that delivers messages to a future version of the user at a chosen date.

## The water types

**Still Water.** People who process quietly before responding. They observe carefully, take time before speaking, and tend to think through things fully before sharing them. Composure is often mistaken for detachment.

**Running Water.** People who process by doing and expressing. They think out loud, move toward things rather than waiting, and often understand how they feel by talking or writing it out.

**Deep Water.** People with strong intuition who operate mostly beneath the surface. They pick up on things others miss, find it hard to explain how they know what they know, and need time to bring internal experience into words.

**Restless Water.** People who are honest about being in transition. They sit with uncertainty rather than forcing resolution, and are often more comfortable with open questions than with settled answers.

**Tidal Water.** People who move in patterns and cycles. They return to things, recognise recurring feelings or situations, and understand themselves through what comes back rather than what is new.

**Spring Water.** People oriented toward beginning again. They bring openness to new situations, recover well from difficulty, and tend to move toward connection and possibility.

**Underground Water.** People who protect what matters to them by keeping it private until they are certain it is safe. They sustain a great deal beneath the surface and are selective about what they share and with whom.

## Story world

The Well includes a growing set of branching stories rooted in Black, African, and diaspora histories. The stories are not educational supplements. They are the emotional centre of the app.

The goal is not to turn history into a lesson, but to let users move through it as feeling, choice, inheritance, and consequence. History enters through character and decision, not exposition.

Current threads:

- A present-day family story about return, silence, and a well behind the house
- A story set in present-day Accra, where a grandmother's death reveals what the family has not spoken
- A story set during the War of the Golden Stool in Asante, 1900
- A story set in Benin City the year before the British Punitive Expedition, centred on a brass caster and his daughter
- A story set during the Women's War of 1929 in southeastern Nigeria, centred on a mother and daughter

Planned threads include Caribbean diaspora stories and Windrush-era London.

## XR direction

The prototype establishes the emotional and narrative logic of the app. The proposed XR extension translates that logic into immersive space.

In the XR experience, the user's water type shapes the environment they move through. A Still Water user enters a different space than a Running Water user. The branching story engine becomes a spatial experience: choices alter the room, the light, the sound, the objects present. Ancestral memory becomes architecture.

The target platform is a room-scale VR or mixed reality environment, designed to be experienced as a solo piece with a duration of approximately twenty minutes.

The residency would be used to prototype the spatial and technical translation of the water type system, develop the XR version of at least one story thread, and test the relationship between user identity, narrative choice, and immersive environment.

---

## Technical notes

The current prototype is a single-file HTML mobile web app.

- No framework, no build step, no external dependencies
- Designed mobile-first, runs offline once loaded
- Fully functional across onboarding, journaling, stories, cultural sphere, breathing tools, and settings
- Built to be saved to a phone home screen as a lightweight app experience

## About

**The Well** was conceived and built by Ihomehe (Golda Ruth) Agbebaku, a writer, filmmaker, performer, and MFA candidate in Creative Writing at the University of British Columbia. She is Editor-in-Chief of PRISM International literary magazine and a community organiser in Vancouver's ballroom scene.

The project is part of a broader creative practice focused on memory, cultural identity, Black interiority, and tools for communities that have often been asked to make themselves legible before being cared for.
