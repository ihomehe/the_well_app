The Well
The Well is a mobile-first wellness and cultural identity app built around voice, reflection, story, and ancestral memory.

It is designed for Black and diaspora users who want a wellness space that does not ask them to translate themselves first.

The app combines journaling, emotional check-ins, breathing tools, cultural storytelling, and a water-based identity system. Instead of treating wellness as a generic routine, The Well approaches self-knowledge as something shaped by memory, culture, language, body, and belonging.


Core idea
The Well asks:

What would a wellness app feel like if it began with recognition instead of correction?

Many wellness tools focus on productivity, mindfulness, or mood tracking in ways that can feel culturally neutral, but rarely are. The Well starts from a different place. It is built around the belief that emotional life is not separate from history, migration, family, spirituality, culture, or the body.

At the centre of the app is a water type system. Users discover the kind of water they are: Still, Running, Deep, Restless, Tidal, Spring, or Underground. These are not rigid personality types. They are emotional rhythms. They describe how a person processes, protects, speaks, remembers, and returns to themselves.


Main features
Water Type Reading
A short onboarding quiz introduces users to their water type. The experience is designed to feel less like assessment and more like recognition.

Each water type shapes the user's prompts, language, story experience, and emotional check-ins across the app.
Speak
A voice and written journaling space. Users can record or write entries in response to prompts shaped by their water type and current emotional state.
Listen
A curated story and audio space for diaspora voices. Users can read, listen, save, or quietly mark stories that resonate.
Wander
A cultural knowledge sphere organised by region and theme. Wander gathers practices, philosophies, histories, language, folktales, and ways of living from across the African diaspora and the Global South.
Play
A reflective games section, including emotional mapping tools, daily questions, breath-based play, memory prompts, and story experiences.
The Well Speaks
An interactive branching story engine where choices shape the ending. The story does not label the user's choices or explain what they mean. Instead, it quietly reflects the user back to themselves through narrative.
Breathe
Grounding and breathing exercises framed through water, body, and return rather than generic wellness language.
Future Self
A letter-writing feature that allows users to send notes to a future version of themselves.


The water types
Still Water
Still Water holds before it speaks. It notices, absorbs, and lets meaning settle before anything is released.
Running Water
Running Water understands through movement. It processes by speaking, doing, trying, and letting the current carry things forward.
Deep Water
Deep Water senses what has not yet become language. It knows before it can explain.
Restless Water
Restless Water is honest about being between things. Not lost, but searching. The uncertainty is the truth.
Tidal Water
Tidal Water moves in cycles. It returns, remembers, recognises patterns, and changes through repetition.
Spring Water
Spring Water begins again. It carries tenderness, emergence, and the courage to reach toward newness.
Underground Water
Underground Water protects what is precious. It sustains things quietly, beneath the surface, until they are ready to rise.


Story world
The Well includes a growing set of branching stories rooted in Black, African, and diaspora histories.

Current and planned story threads include:

A present-day family story about return, inheritance, silence, and a well behind the house
A story set in Accra, where a grandmother's death reveals what the family has not spoken
A story inspired by the War of the Golden Stool in Asante history
A story set in Benin City before the British Punitive Expedition
A story inspired by the Women's War of 1929 in southeastern Nigeria
Future Caribbean and Windrush-era diaspora threads

The historical stories are grounded in documented events, but the characters and emotional journeys are fictionalised. The goal is not to turn history into a lesson, but to let users move through it as feeling, choice, inheritance, and consequence.


Technical notes
The current prototype is a single-file HTML mobile web app.

No framework
No build step
No external dependencies
Designed mobile-first
Can run offline once loaded
Built to be saved to a phone home screen as a lightweight PWA-style experience

The app currently includes onboarding, water type logic, journaling, story feeds, breathing tools, a cultural sphere, interactive story sections, settings, privacy options, and user activity tracking within the prototype environment.


Project direction
Future development will focus on:

Connecting quiz results more deeply to story choices
Expanding the story engine
Adding more diaspora and Global South cultural entries
Developing the Listen tab into a space for community-submitted stories
Building a stronger privacy and consent system
Moving from prototype to a more scalable app architecture


About
The Well was conceived and built by Ihomehe (Golda Ruth) Agbebaku, a writer, filmmaker, performer, and MFA candidate in Creative Writing at the University of British Columbia.

The project is part of a broader creative practice focused on memory, cultural identity, Black interiority, storytelling, and tools for communities that have often been asked to make themselves legible before being cared for.
